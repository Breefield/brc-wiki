{% trans "A community knowledge commons" %}

{% blocktrans %}What if there was one place to go to learn everything about your community? Whether building transparency, accountability and trust between government and residents, appreciating the beauty and the quirks of a community’s unique history, or helping each other navigate invaluable local resources, each month over 400,000 people around the world use LocalWiki to learn about and improve their community.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Free, open, and non-profit" %}

{% blocktrans %}All content on LocalWiki is free for anyone to read, copy and reuse. The code powering the LocalWiki platform is open-source, and our privacy policy is simple and transparent. We are non-commercial, do not run any advertisements, and are a 501(c)(3) non-profit.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "A collaborative storytelling tool" %}

{% blocktrans %}Everybody holds a piece of the puzzle of what it’s like to live in their community. Unlike a news site or blog, anyone can add or modify pages. LocalWiki is what you and your neighbors make of it. Tell the story of your community, collaboratively!{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "An API for your city" %}

{% blocktrans %}LocalWiki is the universal open local knowledge platform upon which countless civic and locally-relevant applications will be built.
